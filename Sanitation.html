Some sanitation practices

  • Washing your hands is one of the most effective ways to reduce the spread of disease. You should especially wash your hands before and after you eat, after going to the bathroom and when you come into contact with someone who is sick. In order to achieve effective results, make sure you wash your hands properly.
  • Using a toilet to keep feces separate from people
  • Washing bodies regularly to maintain physical cleanliness
  • Cover Your Mouth When Your Cough or Sneeze: This is extremely important to avoid spreading germs to people around you
  • Water should be treated with alum (phitkari) before storage
  • Properly dispose and treat sewage before releasing them.When water is exposed to untreated sewage it becomes a breeding ground for parasites and water-borne diseases such as cholera, dysentery, e.coli, and salmonella
