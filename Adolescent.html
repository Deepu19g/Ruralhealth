Adolescent Health

Healthy Eating practices:

  • Eating breakfast: A healthy breakfast is an important part of a balanced diet and provides some of the vitamins and minerals required for good health
  • Aim to eat fruits and vegetables a day
  • Drink enough fluids: One should aim to drink at least six to eight glasses of fluids a day,
  • Eating healthy food: Foods such as whole meal bread, beans, wholegrain breakfast cereals, fruit and vegetables.
  • Being Underweight: Not eating a balanced diet or restricting food intake can lead to deficiency of important nutrients in the body. This can cause health problems
  • Being Overweight: Foods rich in fat and sugar are high in calories. Try to eat lesser foods that are high in fat and sugar

Side effects of alcohol

  • Lack of co-ordination and sluggish reflexes
  • Reduced concentration
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Flushed appearance
  • Blurred vision and slurred speech
  • Long term consumption of alcohol may lead to diseases like cirrhosis of the liver, cancer etc
  • Reproductive issues, such as sexual impotence or a reduction in fertility

Warning signs of drug addiction

  • Eyes become reddish and teens generally complaints about their health, such as being overly tired
  • Teen may show lack of interest in school or there may be a drop in grades.
  • Other signs stains on teen’s clothing, hands, or face.

Ways to help an addicted child

  • Talk to the child early about his or her behavior change.
  • Keep teen busy with meaningful activities, such as sports, or other groups
  • Keep talking with teen. Praise the child for even the little things he or she does well
  • Know your child’s friends. Having friends who avoid cigarettes, alcohol, and drugs may be your teen’s best protection from substance abuse
